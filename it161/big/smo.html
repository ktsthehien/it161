Social Media Optimization (SMO)

Social Network Optimization (SMO) is the use of social media networks to increase an organization's product awareness, brand, and online presence. Many different social media platforms can be used for digital marketing, including Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, Snapchat, YouTube, and Pinterest. In general, social optimization refers to optimization. a website and its content to encourage more users to use and share links to the website on social media and networking sites (Kenton).

Companies use SMO to strategically create interesting online content. According to Will Kenton, “Sharing tools on social media platforms allow users to share content on the Internet almost instantaneously. Because of this, many companies try to create content that users will pass along to their friends and connections. This strategy, called viral marketing, attempts to achieve a broader reach by getting engaged users of social media platforms to share content rather than relying on users to find the content on their own”. The content can be well-written text to attractive digital photos or video clips that encourages and entices people to engage with a website and then share this content, via social media (Kenton).

One of the SMO strategies is keyword research. Keyword research is about identifying the terms and topics that the target audience are using, so you can create content that's right for them. According to Dominique Jackson, “ You need to know what topics, hashtags and keywords your target audience uses so you can share content that’s relevant to their interests. Keyword research will also help you learn which hashtags and phrases to use in your social media posts so you have a higher chance of being found when people search for content relevant to your brand”(Jackson).

One of the tips for social optimization is to use hashtags. The hashtag will make the posts more accessible and make the content easier to find. Dominique noted that “ hashtags have become synonymous with social media marketing. Instagram, Twitter, Facebook and pretty much all other top social media sites integrate hashtags in some fashion. They make it easy to track trending topics, categorize social media posts and they can be entertaining”(Jackson)
